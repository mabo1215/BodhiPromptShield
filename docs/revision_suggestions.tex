\section*{Review Summary}

This manuscript presents an updated privacy-preserving text classification framework built around Word2Vec-style document representations, differential privacy, and a distribution-driven classification margin. The paper positions itself against an earlier DBN/PB-based framework by replacing the learned privacy boundary with a theoretically motivated high-dimensional ring/ellipsoid distribution and an internal classification center. While the manuscript appears to move away from the earlier DBN-based design, the present submission still shows substantial conceptual, structural, and textual continuity with the prior work. In its current form, the manuscript raises a nontrivial risk of \emph{redundant publication / self-overlap} and also falls short of top-tier standards in rigor, novelty isolation, mathematical clarity, and experimental validation.

\section*{Major Concerns}

\subsection*{1. High risk of redundant-publication / self-overlap relative to prior work}
The manuscript explicitly frames itself as a contrast to an earlier method based on \emph{Deep Belief Network (DBN)} and a \emph{Privacy Boundary (PB)}, while retaining the same broad problem setting, application scenario, privacy motivation, text-classification pipeline, and several closely related modeling components. This creates a serious editorial risk unless the paper clearly and explicitly states:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item what the prior paper contributed,
    \item what is retained here,
    \item what is removed,
    \item what is fundamentally new, and
    \item why the present manuscript is not merely a rewritten extension.
\end{enumerate}

\noindent \textbf{Required revision:}
Add a dedicated subsection in the Introduction titled:
\[
\texttt{Differences from Prior DBN/PB-based Work}
\]
and include a precise itemized comparison table with columns:
\[
\{\text{Aspect},\ \text{Prior Work},\ \text{This Work},\ \text{Why the difference matters}\}.
\]

\noindent The comparison must explicitly cover:
\begin{itemize}
    \item privacy mechanism,
    \item boundary construction,
    \item role of DBN,
    \item sequence/dictionary mining method,
    \item theoretical claims,
    \item datasets,
    \item experiments,
    \item reused versus newly added material.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{2. Novelty is asserted, but not isolated with sufficient rigor}
The manuscript claims that the main novelty lies in replacing the learned DBN/PB boundary with a distribution-driven margin derived from a high-dimensional ring/ellipsoid structure and an internal classification center. However, this novelty is not isolated experimentally or theoretically. At present, the manuscript does not convincingly answer:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Why is the ring/ellipsoid hypothesis the right inductive bias?
    \item Under what assumptions does it hold?
    \item How sensitive is the method to violations of that assumption?
    \item How much performance gain is attributable to the new boundary, rather than other pipeline changes?
\end{enumerate}

\noindent \textbf{Required revision:}
Include an ablation section that separately compares:
\begin{align}
&\text{(A) DBN/PB boundary}, \\
&\text{(B) random/no boundary}, \\
&\text{(C) internal-classification-center only}, \\
&\text{(D) ring/ellipsoid + internal center}, \\
&\text{(E) ring/ellipsoid + internal center + GDIFSEA}.
\end{align}
Without this decomposition, the novelty claim remains under-supported.

\subsection*{3. The mathematical development is not yet top-tier reliable}
Several definitions, transitions, and equations are presented in a way that is difficult to verify formally. In particular:
\begin{itemize}
    \item the presentation of differential privacy is imprecise;
    \item notation is inconsistent;
    \item some equations are introduced without a clear derivation path;
    \item the relationship among margin geometry, Hessian-based arguments, and privacy noise is not cleanly justified;
    \item the ring/ellipsoid distribution claim is only loosely connected to the downstream classifier.
\end{itemize}

\noindent \textbf{Required revision:}
Rewrite the theory section into three clean layers:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Representation layer:} assumptions on document vectors;
    \item \textbf{Geometric layer:} why concentration implies a shell/ring structure;
    \item \textbf{Learning layer:} how the classifier and privacy mechanism exploit that geometry.
\end{enumerate}

\noindent Also add a formal proposition of the form:
\begin{quote}
Under assumptions \( \mathcal{A}_1,\ldots,\mathcal{A}_k \), the standardized document vectors concentrate with high probability in a shell
\[
r_1 \leq \|z\|_2 \leq r_2,
\]
where \(r_1, r_2\) are functions of the dimension and covariance structure.
\end{quote}

\noindent Then explain exactly how this proposition changes the classifier design.

\subsection*{4. The relation between privacy guarantee and algorithmic implementation is unclear}
The manuscript repeatedly states that privacy is achieved by \(\varepsilon\)-differential privacy and Laplace noise, but the exact release mechanism, sensitivity definition, adjacency relation, and composition accounting are not sufficiently specified. For a top-tier venue, this is a major issue because privacy claims must be operational, not rhetorical.

\noindent \textbf{Required revision:}
Add a subsection titled:
\[
\texttt{Formal Privacy Mechanism and Adjacency Definition}
\]
with explicit definitions of:
\begin{align}
&\text{dataset adjacency},\\
&\Delta f = \max_{D \sim D'} \|f(D)-f(D')\|_1,\\
&\mathcal{M}(D)=f(D)+\eta,\quad \eta \sim \mathrm{Lap}\!\left(\frac{\Delta f}{\varepsilon}\right).
\end{align}

\noindent Then state clearly:
\begin{itemize}
    \item what \(f\) is in the actual implementation,
    \item where noise is injected,
    \item whether privacy applies to training, inference, or released outputs,
    \item whether multiple releases require composition accounting.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{5. Experimental evidence is broad but not decisive}
The manuscript includes multiple datasets and figures, but the empirical story is not yet persuasive by top-tier standards. The current experiments do not fully establish:
\begin{itemize}
    \item superiority over strong modern baselines,
    \item statistical significance,
    \item robustness to hyperparameters,
    \item sensitivity to shell-assumption mismatch,
    \item reproducibility.
\end{itemize}

\noindent \textbf{Required revision:}
At minimum, add:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item strong baselines (e.g., TF--IDF+linear SVM, CNN/RNN baseline, a transformer baseline, and the prior DBN/PB model if available),
    \item mean \(\pm\) standard deviation over multiple runs,
    \item significance tests,
    \item runtime and memory analysis,
    \item privacy--utility curves under matched settings.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection*{6. The paper still contains substantial writing-quality issues}
The manuscript is currently not polished enough for a top-tier review process. There are many sentences with grammatical problems, vague claims, and informal transitions. In several places, the prose obscures the real contribution.

\noindent \textbf{Required revision:}
Perform a full technical-language rewrite. In particular:
\begin{itemize}
    \item remove conversational or ambiguous wording;
    \item replace claim-like statements with precise, testable statements;
    \item ensure every symbol is defined once and used consistently;
    \item avoid repeating the same motivation across multiple subsections.
\end{itemize}

\section*{Self-Overlap Risk Mitigation (Ethically Required)}

\noindent The following actions are strongly recommended before submission:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Cite the prior DBN/PB paper prominently in the Introduction and Related Work.
    \item Add a dedicated ``relationship to prior work'' subsection.
    \item Re-write all inherited background text from scratch rather than lightly editing prior wording.
    \item Do not reuse prior figures/tables unless clearly cited and justified.
    \item Explicitly state whether any datasets, experiments, or sections overlap with the previous paper.
\end{enumerate}

\noindent A safe disclosure sentence would be:
\begin{quote}
This manuscript builds on our prior DBN/PB-based framework, but differs from it in three fundamental ways: (i) the privacy boundary is no longer learned by DBN; (ii) the proposed classifier is driven by a theoretically motivated shell/ring geometry and internal classification center; and (iii) the sequence/dictionary component is reformulated as GDIFSEA without a learned privacy boundary. All overlapping background material has been rewritten and the new experiments are designed to isolate these changes.
\end{quote}

\section*{Section-by-Section Revision Checklist}

\subsection*{Introduction}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Shorten the generic NLP motivation.
    \item Move immediately to the exact problem: privacy-preserving text classification under incomplete annotation and class imbalance.
    \item Add a transparent paragraph disclosing the relationship to the prior DBN/PB work.
    \item Rewrite the contribution list so each item is one sentence with one verifiable claim.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Related Work}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Split into: privacy-preserving NLP, DP for text models, SVM/privacy methods, and prior DBN/PB work.
    \item Avoid using the prior work merely as a strawman; compare fairly.
    \item Add a table contrasting assumptions, privacy mechanism, and computational cost.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Method}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Separate assumptions from algorithm steps.
    \item Provide pseudocode for GDIFSEA and for the full training pipeline.
    \item Clarify the role of the internal classification center.
    \item Define all symbols before use.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Theory}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Replace informal geometric claims with propositions/lemmas.
    \item State all assumptions explicitly.
    \item Remove or rewrite any equation whose derivation is incomplete.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Experiments}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Add strong baselines.
    \item Add ablations isolating the source of gains.
    \item Add statistical tests.
    \item Add reproducibility details: hyperparameters, train/val/test split, seeds, compute budget.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Conclusion}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Temper overly broad claims.
    \item State limitations honestly.
    \item Clarify what remains unproven theoretically.
\end{itemize}

\section*{Recommendation}

At present, I would recommend \textbf{Major Revision} at best, and possibly \textbf{Reject} if the submission does not transparently address the overlap with the prior DBN/PB paper. The manuscript has the basis for a publishable idea, but it must substantially improve novelty isolation, mathematical rigor, writing quality, and disclosure of its relationship to prior work.
