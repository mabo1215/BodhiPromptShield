\section*{References Patch for \texttt{main.tex}}
\label{sec:references_patch}

This file provides direct citation patch instructions for the current prompt-privacy version of the manuscript. The goal is to make the paper better grounded in the literature while keeping citations aligned with the new story. The references below use keys already available in \texttt{references.bib} whenever possible.

\subsection*{Patch Rule 1: Introduction}
Add or verify the following citations in the corresponding paragraphs.

\paragraph{Paragraph on LLM/VLM agents and tool use}
At the sentence discussing agent systems, tool-augmented reasoning, and multi-component execution, add:
\begin{verbatim}
\cite{yao2022react,schick2023toolformer}
\end{verbatim}
Recommended sentence form:
\begin{verbatim}
Large language models (LLMs) and vision-language models (VLMs) have become the core reasoning engines of modern intelligent assistants and agent systems, including tool-using and reasoning-acting pipelines \cite{yao2022react,schick2023toolformer}.
\end{verbatim}

\paragraph{Paragraph on privacy exposure through model interaction}
At the sentence about raw prompts being exposed during inference, storage, or integration, add:
\begin{verbatim}
\cite{carlini2021extracting,mireshghallah2023privacy}
\end{verbatim}
Recommended sentence form:
\begin{verbatim}
Directly sending unprotected prompts to LLM- or VLM-based systems may lead to unnecessary privacy exposure, especially in settings where memorization, logging, or downstream processing can reveal sensitive content \cite{carlini2021extracting,mireshghallah2023privacy}.
\end{verbatim}

\paragraph{Paragraph on agent risk and prompt injection / indirect leakage}
At the sentence about privacy risk being amplified in agent settings, add:
\begin{verbatim}
\cite{perez2022ignore,greshake2023not}
\end{verbatim}
Recommended sentence form:
\begin{verbatim}
In agent settings, the privacy risk may be amplified because sensitive content can propagate across multiple components during reasoning and tool invocation, and indirect prompt-mediated attacks can further enlarge the attack surface \cite{perez2022ignore,greshake2023not}.
\end{verbatim}

\paragraph{Paragraph on earlier privacy-preserving NLP}
At the sentence discussing differentially private learning or privacy-preserving text processing, add:
\begin{verbatim}
\cite{dwork2014algorithmic,chaudhuri2011differentially,ma2021privacy}
\end{verbatim}
Recommended sentence form:
\begin{verbatim}
Existing privacy-preserving natural language processing research has mainly focused on training-time data protection, differentially private learning, representation anonymization, or post hoc de-identification \cite{dwork2014algorithmic,chaudhuri2011differentially,ma2021privacy}.
\end{verbatim}
